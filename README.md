**Task 1: Exploring and Visualizing the Iris Dataset**

**What I Did**
In this task, I worked with the Iris dataset and performed basic data exploration and visualization using Python. I loaded the dataset, checked its structure, viewed sample records, and analyzed the data through different visualizations including scatter plots, histograms, and box plots.

 **My Observations**
- The dataset contains 150 flower samples belonging to three different species: Setosa, Versicolor, and Virginica.
- The scatter plot shows that Setosa is clearly separated from the other two species based on petal measurements.
- The histogram helped me understand the distribution of sepal length values in the dataset.
- The box plot showed the spread of the data and highlighted a few possible outliers.
- Petal measurements appear to be more useful than sepal measurements for distinguishing between flower species.

 **Conclusion**
This task helped me learn how to explore a dataset and create visualizations to better understand the data. Using graphs made it easier to identify patterns, distributions, and differences between the flower species in the Iris dataset.

 **Task 4: Predicting Insurance Claim Amounts**

**What I Did**

In this task, I worked on predicting insurance claim amounts using customer information such as age, BMI, and smoking status. I first explored the dataset and then created visualizations to understand how different factors affect insurance charges.

After analyzing the data, I trained a Linear Regression model to estimate insurance claim amounts and evaluated its performance using standard regression metrics.

**My Observations**

- Customers with higher BMI values generally tend to have higher insurance charges.
- Smoking has a significant impact on insurance claim amounts, as smokers usually have much higher charges than non-smokers.
- Age also plays an important role, with older customers often having higher insurance costs.
- The visualizations helped identify the relationship between personal factors and insurance charges.
- The Linear Regression model was able to capture the overall trend in the data and make reasonable predictions.


### Conclusion

This task helped me understand the basics of regression modeling and how machine learning can be used to predict numerical values. I also learned how to visualize data, analyze feature relationships, and evaluate model performance using regression metrics.


**Task 2: Credit Risk Prediction**

### What I Did

In this task, I worked on predicting whether a loan application would be approved or not based on applicant information. I created a dataset containing features such as applicant income, loan amount, credit history, and education level.

To make the project more realistic, I introduced some missing values and then handled them using appropriate techniques. After cleaning the data, I performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) and created visualizations to better understand the relationships between different variables.

Finally, I trained a Logistic Regression model to predict loan approval status and evaluated its performance using accuracy score and a confusion matrix.

### My Observations

- Applicants with a good credit history had a higher chance of loan approval.
- Applicant income and loan amount showed different distributions across the dataset.
- Graduate applicants appeared to receive loan approvals more frequently than non-graduates.
- Handling missing values improved the quality and consistency of the dataset.
- Credit history was one of the most important factors influencing loan approval decisions.

### Conclusion

This task helped me understand the complete workflow of a classification problem, including data cleaning, handling missing values, data visualization, model training, and performance evaluation. I also gained practical experience with Logistic Regression and learned how different applicant characteristics can influence loan approval decisions.
